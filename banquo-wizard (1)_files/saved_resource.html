ghost light /ɡōst līt/ noun

A single bulb left burning center-stage in an empty theatre, so that the space is never truly dark. A safety measure, and a superstition: theatres are said to be haunted, and the light keeps the ghosts at bay.

A Dossier

GhostLight AI.

The assistant that sits quietly in every Banquo production. It speaks theatre. It keeps the candle burning while you make the art.

i.

Speaks your scenes.

"When does Act II, Scene 3 break?" Ask it like a stage manager would. It answers in the language of the script, not the language of the database.

Built on your Scene Builder. Knows every act, cue, and cast member by name.
ii.

Drafts document templates.

Donor acknowledgments, tax receipts, volunteer thank-yous, rehearsal reports, all in your voice.

Learns your house style. Never sends the same letter twice.
iii.

Watches the budget.

Flags variance before it becomes a problem. Tells you Props is 12% over before the designer does. Gently.

No surprises at the next board meeting.
iv.

Recommends cues.

Learns your show's rhythm across rehearsals. Suggests light, sound, and practical cues where the Scene Builder implies them, so your cue sheet starts half-written.

You still call the show. It just stops staring at blank rows.
v.

Speaks theatre.

Not "user accounts" and "task lists." Companies, casts, rehearsals, strikes, pickups, load-ins. GhostLight understands what you mean when you say "the Tuesday room," "the strike crew," or "the dry tech." Every prompt, every answer, in the language of the art.

GhostLight ships when Banquo does. Take a seat and we'll let you know.
